Frosta Car Ad

Web Banner

Background

Catching Audience's Attention

Tools Used: Photoshop

Every website earns its revenue through online advertisments which are also known as web banners. In order to grab an instant attention of visitors, I decided to create one of the common types of banner -- car -- to showcase the foundation of typography and layout skill.

Process

Step 1 - Planning

Like everything else, planning and researching are important and helpful parts of the process to achieve the desired result. I started off with writing down necessary contents such as name, price, and calls-to-action button, so I do not forget to include later. Then I make a rough sketch on the how banner is going to look using a reference. I also created a logo of the fictional company.

Step 2 - Basic Layout

Instead of trying to going into details on each element, I did a basic layout of an overall composition in order to get a clear idea of what to work on in the order. It also helped me not to get distracted from nitpicking on minor details. The company was named after the North Pole and specializes in manufacturing cars that can be driven in an extreme condition.

Step 3 - Adding Effects

The frost effect was achieved by mainly Filters: Plastic Wrap and Glass along with Blending Mode. Debris was created using a Custom Brush and Motion Blur were used to help the car appear running. Smart Object were applied on certain layers for a non-destructive method. The logo, which was created in illustrator, was imported into Photoshop and Layer Effects were applied.

Reflections

Result

For my first attempt to make a ready-to-use web banner, I am pretty satisfied as I was able to utilize my typography skill.

What I learned

When I look at banners and posters, I tend to think it would not take much time to make them. Actually, it was easier said than done. There is more than meets the eye as a good amount of time is invested in coming up with contents, choosing colors and typefaces, and laying out in a readable and an eye-catching manner.

Next Steps

As I am getting comfortble with utilizing tools in Photoshop in a various way, I will continue to practice to not only speed up my process, but also to enhance the quality of my works.
