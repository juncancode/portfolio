Nintendo Switch

Vector Graphics

Background

Creating a realistic graphic

Tools Used: Illustrator

We were given an assignment to make a realistic illustration using a reference photo of mechanical objects such as clock, camera, and game consoles. The main goal was to get familiar with tools -- gradient, pathfinder, and color swatch. I decided to replicate one of the popular game consoles, Nintendo Switch.

Process

Step 1 - Basic Shapes

Started off with basic compound shapes before applying any colors. I was encouraged to avoid using pen tool as much possible and use organic shapes -- rectangle, ellipse, and triangle -- instead to create a natural look. Offset Path and Interect were used often.

Step 2 - Applying Colors

After organizing layers by separating different parts of the console, I created color swatches by sampling colors from a refrence photos. Then I gradually moved onto adding details by applying gradients and effects (mainly Gussian Blur) to depict the shading and lighting.

Step 3 - Polishing

Polishing step included reducing unncessary anchor points using Simplify, aligning every shape symmetrically, and checking whether every shape were expanded. Lastly, subtle reflection was added through gradient mask to give a rich feeling.

Reflections

Result

I am satisfied with overall look and feel as I managed to create various resemblance in terms of shapes, shading, and lighting.

What I learned

Throughout this project, I learned what it takes to create a realistic vector graphics, which require attention to detail and technical methods. Tweaking with the Gradient Tool along with its direction and trying different effects to acheive subtle shadows were a valuable experience.

Next Steps

The D-Pad parts needs more depth as well as enhanced lighting. The challenging part was to create divided lines that creates the depth for the center of the D-Pad. Also the bottom of each controllers needs to be darkened distinctly.
